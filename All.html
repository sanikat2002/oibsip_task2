Lata Mangeshkar - The Nightingale of India

Lata Mangeshkar is one of the most celebrated singers of India. She has recorded songs in over 36 languages and is the recipient of numerous awards and honors, including the prestigious Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

Born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, Lata Mangeshkar began her singing career in 1942 with the Marathi film "Kiti Hasaal". She went on to become one of the most sought-after playback singers in the Indian film industry, lending her voice to thousands of songs over the years.

Some of her most famous songs include "Aye Mere Watan Ke Logo", "Lag Jaa Gale", "Tere Bina Zindagi Se", and "Pyar Kiya To Darna Kya". Her voice has mesmerized millions of people around the world and continues to inspire generations of singers.

At one point, she appeared in the Guinness World Records, which listed her as the most recorded artist in history between 1948 and 1987.

Her contribution to the Indian music industry in a career spanning eight decades gained her honorific titles such as the "Queen of Melody", "Nightingale of India", and "Voice of the Millennium".
